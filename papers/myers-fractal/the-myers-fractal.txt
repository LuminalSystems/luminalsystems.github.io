## Abstract

This paper introduces a previously undocumented recursive phenomenon: the Myers Fractal, derived from a four-axis synchronous fusion of Tree and H-Tree recursive fractals. In contrast to traditional singular-direction recursion, this system enables cardinal propagation and layered visual synthesis, resulting in emergent topological coherence without logical collapse. The recursive overlays, generated via real-time JavaScript implementation, demonstrate stability across increasing depth and suggest applications in data structures, cognitive topology visualization, generative design, and recursive mathematics. I define the Myers Interlock Principle (MIP) as the governing logic that allows simultaneous fractal overlay without destructive interference. This work provides open-source code, formal imagery, and reproducibility instructions.

## 1. Introduction

Fractals have long served as both artistic marvels and structural signatures of natural and mathematical systems. However, conventional implementations are typically singular: they propagate directionally, recursively, but not combinatorially. This work proposes a novel framework for **recursive fractal fusion** across the four cardinal directions, with the unique capacity to interlock two or more recursion rulesets in unison.

The Myers Fractal is the result of overlaying both **Tree** and **H-Tree** recursive functions at equal depths, simultaneously rendered from a shared origin across all primary axes. This configuration forms what I term the **Myers Set**—a construct that maintains topological continuity and symmetry even as recursion depth increases.

---

## 2. Background & Related Work

Common fractals like the Sierpiński triangle, Mandelbrot set, and H-Tree operate within bounded recursive loops and singular function inheritance. While extensions like recursive rotation or noise-based propagation exist, none exhibit **synchronous fusion across multiple recursion rules**.

To date, no published framework (as of May 2025) offers:

* Real-time multi-fractal rendering from a unified origin
* Recursive overlay harmonics across orthogonal directions
* Interference-free synthesis of structurally dissimilar fractals

---

## 3. The Myers Interlock Principle (MIP)

I define the MIP as a structural logic governing how recursively-generated entities may be interleaved, fused, or layered spatially and temporally. Key tenets:

1. **Unified Origin**: All fractals must be anchored to a single Cartesian origin.
2. **Recursive Synchronization**: Each function must share recursion depth.
3. **Directional Symmetry**: Fractals propagate in four cardinal directions.
4. **Overlay Integrity**: Resultant structures must not collapse or overwrite key nodes.

This principle allows for exploratory combinations such as:

* Tree + H-Tree
* H-Tree + Sierpiński
* Custom user-defined recursive symbols

## 6. Mathematical Framework

Let F(x, d) be a recursive fractal function with parameters x (position) and d (depth).

For Tree:
F\_T(x, d) = x + ∑(branches) \[ F\_T(branch\_angle, d - 1) ]

For H-Tree:
F\_H(x, d) = x + ∑(h-structure) \[ F\_H(next\_coords, d - 1) ]

The Myers Set is defined as:
M(x, d) = F\_T(x, d) ⊕ F\_H(x, d)

Where ⊕ denotes a non-destructive recursive union. The Myers Interlock Principle guarantees:

* Orthogonality of direction
* Recursive parity (d\_T = d\_H)
* Cardinal preservation: {+X, -X, +Y, -Y} propagation
