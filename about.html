Matt D. M.

I am an independent systems thinker, programmer, and builder exploring complex problems across technology, psychology, philosophy, cognition, and computational systems.

My work centers on first-principles reasoning, pattern recognition, and the construction of conceptual frameworks that aim to reveal underlying structure across seemingly unrelated domains. I don't treat disciplines as isolated, because they're not. To me, knowledge is an ocean. Everything is connected to everything in some way, shape, or form. They are systems that can be studied, modeled, and recombined, integrated and unified into one coherent framework or concept.

Operating outside traditional academic pathways, I focus on developing internal coherence before external validation, building models, writing experimental whitepapers, conceptual frameworks, and creating functional prototypes to test ideas in practice.

This work is iterative and evolving. I'm less concerned with definitive answers, and more concerned with refining better questions, clearer abstractions, and more useful representations of complex systems. It is my goal to reduce the complexity to simplicity, to make the implicit, explicit, to find the underlying core axiomatic bases that governs it.

This site serves as a living archive of that process, including papers, tools, code, journals, and exploratory experiments, shared for those who value depth, curiosity, and independent thought. For those who want to know why, must know why. This site serves as the foundation for me, but in the future, I hope serves as a starting point for someone else. I am less interested in what is known, and more interested in how knowledge itself is constructed. How it combines or connects. How knowledge evolves, adapts, and intertwines with what is both known and unknown. I look for the axiomatic base, the logic, the generator of that knowledge.
